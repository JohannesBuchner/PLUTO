Sedov-Taylor blast wave. More...

#include "pluto.h"

Detailed Description

Set the initial condition for a Sedov-Taylor blast wave problem. Ambient density and pressure are constant everywhere and equal to rho0 and p0. While rho0 is an input parameter, p0=1.e-5. A large amount of energy is deposited in a small volume (a line, circle or sphere depending on geometry and dimensions) consisting of 2 or 3 computational zones. This run uses the MHD module with a very large value of plasma beta which essentially means a evolution of blast wave is mostly similar to its hydrodynamic run.

The input parameters read from pluto.ini are labeled as:

  • g_inputParam[ENRG0]: initial energy of the blast wave (not energy density);
  • g_inputParam[DNST0]: initial constant density;
  • g_inputParam[GAMMA]: specific heat ratio.

The available configuration refer to:

  1. Cartesian (2D MHD, equivalent to cylindrical)
